JESUIT, WHO WE ARE?

Find God in all things

The Jesuits, formally known as the Society of Jesus, are a Catholic religious order founded by St. Ignatius of Loyola in 1540. Known for their strong emphasis on education, intellectual rigor, and missionary work, the Jesuits have established numerous schools, universities, and missions around the world. They played a significant role during the Counter-Reformation, defending and spreading Catholic doctrine in response to the Protestant Reformation. Jesuits take vows of poverty, chastity, and obedience, with a special vow of obedience to the Pope regarding mission work. Their influence extends into various fields, including theology, science, and social justice.
